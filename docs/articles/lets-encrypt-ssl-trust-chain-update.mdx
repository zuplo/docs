---
title: Let's Encrypt SSL Trust Chain Update
---

:::note

This is a historical notice. The SSL trust chain change described below was
completed in September 2023.

:::

Let’s Encrypt, one of the certificate authorities (CAs) used by Zuplo,
[announced changes to its chain of trust](https://letsencrypt.org/2023/07/10/cross-sign-expiration)
in July 2023. As a result, certain legacy clients, such as those using Android
7.0, no longer support SSL certificates issued by Let’s Encrypt.

This change impacted a very small number of clients. Across all Zuplo APIs, less
than 1 request per several billion were made by clients affected by this change.

Impacted customers were notified directly at the time. Certificates were
reissued on September 9, 2023.

To support legacy clients that require broader device compatibility, contact
[support@zuplo.com](mailto:support@zuplo.com) about switching to a different
Certificate Authority (Google Trust Services).

## Impacted Legacy Clients

The primary clients impacted by this change were those using Android 7.0 or
earlier. These Android releases came out before 2016 and are no longer supported
by Google.
